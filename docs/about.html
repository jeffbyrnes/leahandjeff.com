How We Met

Jeff and Leah met on a chilly night in February 2012. They didn’t know much about each other, having only seen a few pictures on the dating website, How About We . But Leah thought Jeff had kind eyes and Jeff thought Leah had a great smile. They met at Brick and Mortar, a cocktail joint in Leah’s neighborhood. Jeff arrived early, grabbed two seats at the bar and ordered a whiskey cocktail, a sazerac, to calm his nerves.

Leah arrived a few minutes later and saddled up next to him.

“What are you drinking?” she asked. When Jeff replied, Leah couldn’t help but smile — sazeracs were her favorite cocktail.

Exactly two years later, Jeff and Leah went to their favorite bar, Citizen Public House , to celebrate their two-year anniversary. Jeff ordered two sazeracs and proposed. Of course, Leah said yes.

Jeff and Leah live in Cambridge, MA, where they continuously expand their home bar, and still enjoy the occasional sazerac.

How To Make A Sazerac

“Take a rocks glass, add a dash of absinthe, swirl to coat the glass, then discard. In a separate mixing glass, muddle 1 tablespoon sugar with a splash of soda water. Once dissolved, add two ounces of rye whiskey (preferably 100 proof), two dashes of Peychaud’s bitters, and ice. Stir well until cold. Strain rye & bitters mixture into rocks glass and garnish with a lemon peel.” PUNCH Magazine
